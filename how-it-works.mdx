Think of SpeakLouder as a **workspace per campaign**. A campaign has its own leads, its own voice settings, its own phone flow, and its own automation timeline.

## The journey of one lead

1. **A lead arrives** — Someone fills a form from an ad, or a row appears from a file or an automated feed, depending on campaign type.
2. **The app stores the person** — Name, phone number, and any answers you mapped from the form or file become fields you can use in messages and calls.
3. **Automation runs** — Your scenario waits the times you set, then may place an outgoing call, send an SMS, or both, in the order you drew.
4. **Calls use your assistant** — Incoming and outgoing conversations follow the instructions and voice you configured under **Agent**, using the **Phone number** you linked.
5. **You see history** — On the lead’s card and under automation history you see what already happened and what is scheduled.

## Credits (balance)

Outgoing **voice minutes** and **SMS** consume balance shown under **Credits** in the main menu. The screen shows your current balance and a list of changes (top-ups and usage). Balance is **not** the same as a fixed monthly bundle unless your contract says otherwise — the app shows what is available **now**.

## Account vs campaign settings

- **Account-level** items (profile, security, global integrations) live under the main menu **Settings** and **Integrations**.
- **Campaign-level** items (paused campaign, calling hours, webhook for this campaign) live under **Campaign settings** and **Automation** inside that campaign.

## When to use Help inside the app

Each campaign has a **Help** tab with short cards and a link to **this documentation** (so long explanations are not duplicated in the product). For a deeper walkthrough of the lead panel and account settings, see [Lead detail panel](/guides/lead-panel).
