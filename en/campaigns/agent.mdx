The **Agent** tab is where you set how the agent sounds and what it says. It's the most important part of a campaign.

## Setting it up step by step

A new campaign has no agent configured yet, so instead of empty fields you get a wizard.

<img src="/images/en/agent-onboarding-start.png" alt="Agent configuration start screen" />

Click **Start configuration**. The first question is the one that matters most:

<img src="/images/en/agent-onboarding-language.png" alt="Choosing the agent's spoken language" />

Pick the language your agent will **speak and understand on calls**: English, Spanish, German, French, Italian, Portuguese, Dutch or Polish.

Then answer a dozen short questions about your company and your offer (what you do, what you sell, who you're calling).

<img src="/images/en/agent-onboarding-question.png" alt="Agent configuration wizard, one of the questions" />

From those answers the AI writes the prompt and the agent's opening lines for both outbound and inbound calls. Prefer to write everything yourself? Click **Skip and configure manually** in the top-right corner.

## The configured view

<img src="/images/en/agent-configured.png" alt="Agent tab after configuration" />

- **Voice**: how the agent sounds (see below).
- **Language**: the spoken language, changeable at any time from the dropdown.
- **Prompt configuration**: the instructions for outbound and inbound calls; **Manage** opens the full editor.
- **Callback**: when this is on and a customer asks to be called at another time, the agent schedules the callback itself.
- **Tools**: a connected calendar lets the agent book meetings mid-call. You connect it in [Integrations](/en/integrations).
- **Knowledge base**: documents for the agent to draw on; *coming soon*.

At the top you also get **Test agent** (talk to it in your browser, free of charge) and **Send call** (the agent calls a number you give it).

<Warning>
Changing the language does **not** translate your prompts, they stay exactly as they are. After switching languages, open **Manage prompt** and rewrite them in the new language, otherwise the agent will speak one language while following instructions written in another.
</Warning>

## Choosing a voice

Click **Change** next to Voice:

<img src="/images/en/agent-voice-picker.png" alt="Voice picker with accent filters and previews" />

Filter by accent (American, Australian, British, Dutch, German, Polish) or search by name, hit play to preview each one, then click **Save voice**.

<Tip>
Before you go anywhere near real leads, click **Test agent** and have a practice conversation. It's by far the fastest way to spot what needs fixing in the prompt.
</Tip>
