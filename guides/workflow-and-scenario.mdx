title: Workflow and scenario
description: How the per-campaign automation path works — modules, waits, editor, and limits.
---

Each campaign has **one linear automation path** (a **scenario**). Leads move through **Start → middle steps → End** with **waits** between steps. You configure it under **Automatyzacja** in the campaign menu.

## Automation page (before editing)

On **Automatyzacja** you see:

- **Preview** — a short read-only list of steps (Start, waits, calls/SMS/email, End).
- **Calling hours** — allowed window for outbound actions (save with **Zapisz okno**).
- **Post-call webhook** — optional HTTPS callback after a call where the lead spoke (separate from Make inbound leads).

Use **Historia & Zaplanowane** for run history and scheduled actions. The blue **Edytuj scenariusz** button opens the visual editor (overlay).

## Scenario editor layout

After **Edytuj scenariusz**, the **Scenariusz** overlay shows:

- **Canvas (left)** — vertical flow: **Start** (one per scenario), **middle modules**, **Koniec** (one per scenario). **Segments** between blocks show **how long the system waits** before the next step (seconds, minutes, hours, or days — within product limits).
- **Toolbar** — **Fit view**, **Save scenario**, **Close**, zoom.
- **Right panel** — empty until you select a **module** or click a **wait segment** between modules (hint text explains this).

<img src="/images/app-scenariusz-edytor.png" alt="Scenario editor: Start, Połączenie, Koniec on canvas; details panel empty until selection" />

## Modules you can add

Use **+ Dodaj moduł** at the bottom of the canvas. The menu offers:

| Module (UI) | Role |
| ----------- | ---- |
| **Połączenie** | Outbound **voice call** for this phase. What the assistant **says** is configured under **Agent** (outgoing call). |
| **SMS** | Sends an **SMS** in this step; you edit body and placeholders in the right panel. |
| **E-mail** | **Planned** in the product roadmap; when available, it will send an email step from the scenario. |

**Start** and **Koniec** are fixed ends of the path; you do not add them from the menu.

## Waits between steps

Each arrow between two modules has a **delay** (shown on the canvas, e.g. “10 sek.” or “5 min”). That is how long the system waits after the previous step **completes** before starting the next. Delays can range from **immediate (0)** up to **5 days** per segment.

## Selecting **Połączenie** — right panel

Click a **Połączenie** card (e.g. **Faza 1**). The right panel is the **inspector** for that call phase:

- **Phase name** — label shown in the app and timeline (e.g. “Faza 1” or a custom name).
- **Note** — the **outbound call script** is edited under **Agent**, not here.
- **SMS after answered call** (optional) — if enabled, an SMS may be sent **after** the call when the product treats the lead as sufficiently interested (voicemail / low-interest calls are skipped per the on-screen explanation).
- **Action after call** — e.g. **transfer to a number + email summary**, or **email summary only**; fields depend on the option (transfer number format, summary email).
- **Advanced** — extra options when your campaign exposes them.
- **Save** / **Delete module** — apply changes or remove this step.

## Selecting **SMS** or **E-mail**

When you select an **SMS** module, the right panel includes **message body**, character limits, and **ASCII** handling for SMS (Polish diacritics are normalised as the product explains). **E-mail** steps will expose **subject** and **body** when that module is fully available.

## Limits (what the editor enforces)

These are the main guardrails you may hit while editing:

- **Maximum ~40 nodes** on the canvas (including Start and End).
- **At most 4 × Połączenie** modules — aligned with outbound call attempt limits for no-answer follow-up.
- **SMS body** — up to **160 characters** after normalisation (single GSM segment style).
- **Phase name** — up to **80 characters**.

If save is blocked, the app shows a short message (for example too many **Połączenie** blocks or missing required middle steps).

## Saving and closing

Use **Zapisz scenariusz** to persist the graph. **Zamknij** leaves the overlay without discarding already saved work; if you made edits, save first.
